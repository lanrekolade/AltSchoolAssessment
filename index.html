EWA AGOYIN

Ewa Agoyin is a beloved Yoruba delicacy. It is a richly flavoured pepper sauce served with soft, well cooked beans.

Growing up in Lagos, this was a staple breakfast in many homes including mine and
sometimes we added fried plantain and fried fish and the combination was unforgettable.

The Yoruba truly know how to elevate a simple dish like they did with beans.
This regular stew and then theres this special Yoruba pepper sauce made specially for the beans. A flavour all on its own

Prep Time: 20 mins

Cook Time: 2 hours

Servings: 5 servings

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients List

  • Beans
  • Water
  • Dried Chili
  • Red Pepper Bell
  • Onion
  • Palm oil
  • Seasoning & Spices

How To Prepare

  1. Pick out the specks of dirt from the beans, rinse and drain the beans a couple of times.
  2. Pour the beans in a stockpot, add the water and cook till it becomes very tender for about 2 to 2Â½ hours.
  3. Use a Potato Masher or a wooden spoon to mash the Beans to your preference
  4. Rehydrate the dried Chilies and bell peppers by soaking them in water overnight or pour hot boiling water over them for about 2 hours
  5. Blend the rehydrated Peppers with the onions, ginger, and about a quarter cup of water and set aside.
  6. Preheat the Palm oil in a pan on medium heat until the oil is very hot but not bleached.
  7. Add the sliced onion and let it cook for about 10 to 15 minutes. We want it browned and not black or burnt.
  8. Stir in the blended peppers and cook for about 10 minutes
  9. Add the seasoning cubes, crayfish, and salt to taste.
  10. Leave it to cook for another 5 to 10 minutes; by this time, the color of the pepper would have changed from bright red to dark red. If not, cook for some additional time.
  11. Serve the Sauce over the Ewa Agoyin (Ewa Aganyin) or eat with your Agege Bread and Enjoy!

Additional Note

  • I used Black Eyed Peas for this recipe because it's my preference, feel free to use the Nigerian honey Beans (Ewa Oloyin) if that's what you like.
  • Agoyin tends to dry out fast once it's mashed, and the colder it gets, the drier it appears, so adjust the water level as required.
  • You don't have to bleach the Palm Oil you will use for cooking making the Sauce, once it's very hot it's okay
